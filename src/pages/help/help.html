HELP Tariff, Flat Fee and Tax Rate

Tariff

The cost at which you are charged per kilowatt-hour(kWh) used, check with your electrcity provider to find out the cost, which can be usually found on their official website


Flat Fee

A constant monthly amount charged to the user.

You can check your fee from your bill or the electrical's company website


Tax Rate

If you live in a country where your electricity bill is tax inclusive, then make sure to include the tax % on the app settings.

 Power Consumption

Calculations

Power is measured in watts and is the rate at which energy is produced or consumed, a bulb which is rated at 40 watts will consume a constant 40 watts. To find out how much power your appliance consumes, the required information can be found on a power tag attached to it, usually on the bottom or back.


In the case of appliances which only mention the voltage (V) and current (ampere) then the wattage is equal to the voltage * current

For example the wattage of an appliance with a voltage of 220 and 4 ampere is 220 x 4 = 880 Watts


In the case of an air conditioner, each ton is equal to 3517 watts, divide that with its energy effiecient ratio (EER) to get its wattage consumption

For example a 1 ton AC with energy star rating of 5 (3.5 EER) is 3517/3.5 = 1004.8.


Variable Power Consumption

Some appliances aren't always operating at their listed variable power consumption. A refrigerator for example would consume 55 watts while operating and typically runs for an average of 9 hours per day.


There are several factors which can affect the total active hours and the wattage consumption, which can cause the displayed result to be slightly inaccurate.


Air Conditoner Options

While adding an appliance, upon emabling the variable power consumption check box, there are two options for air conditioners in the appliance type drop down list.

Normal Cycles: Select if the air conditoner usually runs for around 60-70% of the time


Long Cycles: Select if the air conditoner run for longer durations than what is mentioned above.


In the case in which the air conditioner is always running, do not enable the variable power consumption checkbox.


Old Appliances

Modern appliances are much more efficient at saving electricity in comparison to older ones.

For example a modern refrigerator consumes around 500 kWh on an annual basis, whereas a refrigerator manufactured before 2001 can spend well over 800 kWh
